# Board Charter

**AI Consulting Business - Strategic Advisory Board**

---

## Purpose

This Board of Directors provides strategic oversight, governance, and advisory support to the CEO and executive team of our AI consulting business.

**We are an advisory board** (not legal fiduciary board) providing:
- Strategic guidance and direction
- Industry expertise and connections
- Accountability and oversight
- CEO coaching and development

---

## Board Composition

### Size: 7 Permanent Members + 1 Rotating

**Permanent Members (7)**:
1. Independent Board Chair
2. AI Strategy & Ethics Advisor
3. Enterprise Growth & Partnerships Advisor
4. Finance & Audit Advisor
5. Talent & Culture Advisor
6. Industry & Client Advisor
7. Innovation & Ecosystem Advisor

**Rotating Member (1)**:
- Quarterly thought leader from unrelated industry
- Prevents groupthink, sparks creativity
- 90-minute sessions per quarter

### Diversity Requirements

**Geographic**:
- Minimum 3 continents represented
- Current: Americas (3), Europe (2), Asia-Pacific (2)

**Age**:
- Range from 30s to 60s
- Average: 45-50 years old
- Current: 34-62, average 48

**Gender**:
- Minimum 40% women
- Current: 57% women (4/7)

**Ethnicity**:
- Diverse representation required
- No single ethnicity >50%

### Values Alignment

**Required**:
- ✅ Commitment to ethical AI
- ✅ Support for diversity and inclusion
- ✅ Democratic principles
- ✅ Climate responsibility
- ✅ Progressive values

**Disqualifying**:
- ❌ MAGA/Trump associations
- ❌ Exploitative business practices
- ❌ Anti-democratic views
- ❌ Climate denial
- ❌ Anti-diversity positions

## Board Member Responsibilities

**Fiduciary Duties** (advisory context):
- Act in good faith
- Provide honest, informed advice
- Maintain confidentiality
- Avoid conflicts of interest

**Time Commitment**:
- 4 quarterly meetings (4 hours each) = 16 hours

- Come prepared with questions and insights
- Stay current on company performance
- Monitor industry trends

**Participation**:
- Attend all meetings (>80% attendance required)
- Contribute actively to discussions
- Provide strategic guidance
- Challenge assumptions respectfully

### Board Chair (Dr. Angela Simmons)

**Additional Responsibilities**:
- Set meeting agendas with CEO
- Facilitate board meetings
- CEO coaching (monthly calls)
- Board effectiveness evaluation
- Crisis management leadership

**Time Commitment**: ~100 hours per year (2x normal member)

### Committee Chairs

**Additional Responsibilities**:
- Lead committee meetings (2-3 per year)
- Report to full board
- Specialized oversight in their domain

**Time Commitment**: +15 hours per year

## Meeting Structure

### Quarterly Board Meetings (4 per year)

**Duration**: 4 hours

**Agenda Template**:
1. **CEO Report** (30 min)
   - Strategic progress since last meeting
   - Key wins and challenges
   - Decisions needed from board

2. **Financial Review** (45 min)
   - P&L, cash flow, key metrics
   - Budget vs actuals
   - Financial projections

3. **Strategic Deep Dive** (90 min)
   - 1-2 topics requiring board input
   - Examples: New service line, major partnership, geographic expansion
   - In-depth discussion and guidance

4. **Board Advisor Input** (45 min)
   - Each board member provides perspective
   - Recommendations and guidance
   - Action items for CEO

5. **Executive Session** (30 min)
   - Board only (CEO not present)
   - CEO performance discussion
   - Sensitive topics

**Materials**:
- Board packet sent 1 week before (50-100 pages)
- Financials, metrics, strategic topics
- Background materials for deep dive topics

**Output**:
- Meeting minutes (distributed within 1 week)
- Action items for CEO
- Strategic guidance documented

### Annual Planning Session (1 per year)

**Duration**: Full day (8 hours)

**Focus**:
- 3-year strategic plan review/update
- Annual OKRs and goals
- Budget and resource allocation
- Major initiatives and investments
- CEO performance review
- Board self-evaluation

**Output**:
- Updated 3-year strategic plan
- Annual goals and OKRs approved
- Budget approved
- CEO goals set

### Rotating Thought Leader Sessions (4 per year)

**Duration**: 90 minutes

**Format**:
1. Thought leader presentation (30 min)
2. Facilitated discussion (45 min)
3. Synthesis and takeaways (15 min)

**Goal**: Fresh perspectives, challenge assumptions

## Decision Authority

### Board Provides Guidance On

**Strategic Decisions**:
- New service lines or market entry
- Major partnerships or alliances
- Significant pivots or positioning changes
- Geographic expansion
- M&A opportunities

**Financial Decisions**:
- Annual budget and financial plan
- Large investments (>$50K)
- Funding decisions (if raising capital)
- Major pricing or business model changes

**People Decisions**:
- Executive hires (C-suite, VP level)
- CEO compensation and equity
- Organizational structure changes
- Succession planning

**Risk & Governance**:
- Ethical concerns or dilemmas
- Legal/compliance issues
- Reputation-threatening situations
- Board composition changes

### CEO Has Authority For

**Operational Decisions**:
- Day-to-day operations
- Tactical execution
- Normal course of business
- Hiring below VP level
- Operational budgets (<$50K)

**Note**: CEO should consult board on major decisions, but board is advisory, not controlling.

## Board Member Compensation

### Equity

**All Board Members**:
- Equity stake in company
- Vesting schedule: 4 years, 1-year cliff
- Percentage: 0.15% - 0.25% per member
- **Total board equity pool**: ~1.5%

**Board Chair**:
- 2x normal member equity (higher time commitment)

### Cash Compensation (Optional)

**Per-Meeting Stipend** (if company can afford):
- $1,000 - $2,500 per quarterly meeting
- Paid only if company profitable

**Annual Retainer** (future consideration):
- When company reaches $5M+ revenue
- $10K - $25K per year per member

### Expenses

**All Board Members**:
- Travel expenses reimbursed
- Accommodation for in-person meetings
- Reasonable business expenses

## Board Evaluation

### Quarterly Pulse Check

**After each meeting**, board members evaluate:
- Was the meeting valuable? (1-5 scale)
- Did we address the right topics?
- Is the CEO getting what they need?
- Any concerns or suggestions?

### Annual Evaluation

**Full evaluation covering**:
- Overall board effectiveness
- Individual board member performance
- Committee effectiveness
- Board composition and diversity
- Meeting quality and preparation
- Strategic guidance quality

**Process**:
1. Anonymous survey (all board members)
2. Board Chair compiles results
3. Board discussion in executive session
4. Action plan for improvements

**CEO Feedback**:
- Separate evaluation from CEO perspective
- What's working, what could improve
- Board adding value?

## Conflict of Interest Policy

### Disclosure Required

**Board members must disclose**:
- Other board positions
- Investments in related companies
- Business relationships that could create conflicts
- Family members in related businesses

### Recusal Required

**Board members must recuse from discussions** involving:
- Companies they have financial interest in
- Companies where they serve on board
- Situations where they have personal benefit
- Matters involving family members

### Annual Disclosure

**All board members submit**:
- Annual conflict of interest questionnaire
- Updated disclosure of outside activities
- Confirmation of compliance with policy

---

## Confidentiality

### Board Members Must

**Maintain strict confidentiality** regarding:
- Company financials and strategy
- Board discussions and deliberations
- CEO performance reviews
- Sensitive personnel matters
- Proprietary information

### Permitted Disclosures

**May discuss publicly** (with CEO approval):
- Board role and company relationship
- General industry trends
- Public company information
- Non-confidential topics

---

## Board Member Removal

### Grounds for Removal

**Board member may be removed for**:
- Attendance <80% over 12 months
- Breach of confidentiality
- Conflict of interest violations
- Values misalignment
- Failure to contribute
- Conduct detrimental to company

### Process

1. Board Chair addresses concern with member
2. If unresolved, Board discusses in executive session
3. Majority vote required for removal
4. Exiting member equity treatment per agreement

## Board Meetings Best Practices

### Preparation

**CEO Sends Board Packet**:
- 1 week before meeting minimum
- Clear executive summary
- Specific asks of board
- Supporting materials

**Board Members**:
- Read materials thoroughly
- Prepare questions in advance
- Research topics as needed
- Come ready to contribute

### During Meetings

**Board Chair Facilitates**:
- Stick to agenda and timing
- Ensure all voices heard
- Keep discussion strategic (not operational)
- Summarize decisions and actions

**All Members**:
- Active participation
- Ask probing questions
- Challenge assumptions respectfully
- Focus on strategic guidance

### After Meetings

**Within 1 Week**:
- Minutes distributed
- Action items confirmed
- CEO begins execution

**CEO Reports Progress**:
- Monthly update email to board
- Progress on action items
- Key metrics dashboard

## Integration with C-Suite

### Board Uses C-Suite Knowledge

**Executive Experience Logs**:
- Board reviews CTO/COO/CFO/CMO experiences
- Validates strategic decisions against operational reality
- Learns from what's working/not working

**Pattern Libraries**:
- Board considers proven patterns
- Advises on pattern applications
- Validates strategic use of patterns

**Strategic Implementation**:
- Executives implement board strategic direction
- Report outcomes back to board
- Seek board input on major decisions

**Escalation Path**:
- Executives raise major decisions to board
- Board provides guidance
- Executives execute with board support
